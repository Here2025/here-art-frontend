# HERE Demo Script

## Purpose

This document captures the investor/demo walkthrough for HERE. The goal is to explain the product clearly while clicking through the app, so a viewer understands what HERE is, why it matters, and how the current demo proves the larger opportunity.

## 2-Minute Demo Script

HERE is a location-based creative discovery app that helps people find the creative pulse of a city.

When someone opens HERE, they are not just browsing art online. They are discovering what is around them — street art, public art, galleries, museums, performances, fashion, creative events, and hidden cultural places — all connected to real locations.

The first thing users see is Discover. This gives them an immediate way to explore the creative life of a city by category. They can choose Street Art, Public Art, Gallery, Performing Arts, Fashion, Hidden Gem, and more.

From there, the Map becomes the core experience. HERE turns the city into a living creative layer. Instead of creative places being scattered across social media, websites, flyers, and word of mouth, HERE puts them on a map where people can actually find them, save them, visit them, and return to them.

The Events section connects discovery to time. It shows what is happening now, this week, or this weekend — performances, gallery nights, artist-led events, fashion pop-ups, creative markets, and cultural programming.

Profiles add the human layer. Artists, galleries, organizers, collectives, and hosts can have a presence connected to their work, events, and location. This gives creative people better visibility and gives users a way to follow and understand the scene around them.

The Create page supports growth. HERE is profile-first, so users create or claim a profile before adding places or events. That helps support quality, trust, and future verification.

My Space gives users a personal layer where they can save places, like art, follow profiles, and keep track of what they discover.

The larger vision is simple: cities are full of creative work, but that work is often hard to find. HERE gives it a map, a profile layer, an event layer, and a discovery experience built around place.

HERE helps people find the creative pulse of a city — and gives artists, venues, organizers, and cities a better way to be discovered.

## Click-by-Click Demo Walkthrough

### 1. Open on Discover

**Click/Show:** Discover page.

**Say:**
HERE opens with a simple promise: find art where you are. This is the entry point for discovering the creative life of a city through real places, not just online posts.

**Point out:**
- Search bar
- Category buttons
- Featured creative place
- Nearby creative places and related events

### 2. Use Creative Categories

**Click/Show:** Street Art, Public Art, Gallery, Performing Arts, Fashion, Hidden Gem.

**Say:**
Categories help users quickly narrow the city by interest. Someone can look for public art, galleries, performances, fashion, or hidden gems without needing to know where to start.

**Point out:**
The categories are intentionally understandable for everyday users while still supporting artists, galleries, venues, organizers, and city partners.

### 3. Open the Map

**Click/Show:** Map page.

**Say:**
The map is the heart of the product. HERE turns the city into a living creative layer. Creative places become visible through location, so users can actually find, save, visit, and return to them.

**Point out:**
- Category filters
- Search this area
- Selected creative place card
- Save place
- Check in
- Open location

### 4. Open Events

**Click/Show:** Events page.

**Say:**
Creative discovery is also about timing. The Events page shows what is happening now, this week, or this weekend, from performances and gallery nights to artist-led events and fashion pop-ups.

**Point out:**
- Event image
- Date and time
- Location
- Host profile
- Save and open location

### 5. Open a Profile

**Click/Show:** Profile page.

**Say:**
Profiles add the human and organizational layer. Artists, galleries, collectives, organizers, and hosts can have a presence connected to their work, events, and location.

**Point out:**
- Profile identity
- Follow action
- Message action placeholder
- Artworks, events, and journeys tabs

### 6. Open Create

**Click/Show:** Create page.

**Say:**
HERE is profile-first. Before someone adds a place or event, they create or claim a profile. That supports trust, quality, ownership, and future verification.

**Point out:**
- Profile creation
- Artwork/place creation after profile
- Event creation after profile

### 7. Open My Space

**Click/Show:** My Space / Saved.

**Say:**
My Space gives users a personal layer. They can save places, like artwork, follow profiles, and keep track of their creative discoveries.

**Point out:**
This is what turns discovery into repeat use.

## Closing Line

HERE is building location-based infrastructure for creative discovery. It helps people find the creative pulse of a city while giving artists, venues, organizers, and cities a better way to be discovered.

## Demo Notes

- Keep the demo focused on the user journey: Discover, Map, Events, Profile, Create, My Space.
- Avoid overexplaining every button.
- The core message is place-based creative discovery.
- The investor point is that HERE can support consumer discovery, artist visibility, venue and organizer tools, city partnerships, sponsored placements, tourism campaigns, and later ticketing/referral revenue.
