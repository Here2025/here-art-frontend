# HERE Product Foundation

## Core idea

HERE is the gallery for art that lives in the world.

It is a discovery platform for street art, public creativity, local artists, and creative events that deserve visibility but may not fit inside traditional galleries, large venues, or mainstream event platforms.

HERE helps people discover the murals, street art, installations, live music, gallery moments, pop-ups, and hidden cultural experiences happening around them.

## Product promise

HERE turns the city into a gallery.

The app should help users answer:

- What creative beauty is near me right now?
- What street art, public art, or local creative work would I miss if I did not know where to look?
- Which artists, musicians, galleries, collectives, and creative hosts should I be paying attention to?
- What creative events are happening today or this week near me?

## Who HERE is for

### Explorers

People who want to find art, murals, hidden gems, local music, creative events, and cultural experiences by location.

### Street artists and visual artists

Artists whose work lives on walls, buildings, public spaces, and neighborhoods rather than inside a traditional gallery.

### Local musicians and performers

Bands, singers, poets, DJs, dancers, and performers who need visibility for shows and creative moments.

### Galleries, collectives, and hosts

Small galleries, cultural organizations, pop-up hosts, and creative spaces that want more people to discover what they are doing.

## Core app experiences

### 1. Discover

A feed of nearby creative places and events.

Content types:

- Murals
- Street art
- Public installations
- Gallery shows
- Pop-up exhibitions
- Local music
- Creative performances
- Hidden gems

### 2. Map

A location-first view of creative points around the user.

The map should support:

- Artwork pins
- Event pins
- Nearby filtering
- Category filtering
- City and neighborhood exploration
- User location
- Saved places
- Check-ins

### 3. Profiles

Profiles should not be limited to artists. HERE should support:

- Explorer profiles
- Street artist profiles
- Musician profiles
- Performer profiles
- Gallery profiles
- Creative collective profiles
- Event host profiles

### 4. Events

Events should help users discover creative activity that may otherwise be overlooked.

Examples:

- Local band playing tonight
- Gallery opening
- Art walk
- Mural unveiling
- Poetry night
- Pop-up market with artists
- Community performance

### 5. Journeys

Curated routes and collections through public creativity.

Examples:

- Downtown Raleigh murals
- Hidden gems near me
- Black artists to discover
- Date night art walk
- Creative events this weekend
- Street art trail by neighborhood

## Interaction model

Users should be able to:

- Save places
- Like artwork/events
- Check in
- Follow artists/creatives
- Follow galleries or hosts
- Submit artwork or events
- Build personal art trails

## Near-term build priorities

1. Keep the current map and layout stable.
2. Connect PostgreSQL properly so submitted locations persist.
3. Expand the data model beyond artworks to support creative profiles and events.
4. Add a discovery feed that can show both artwork and events.
5. Add real user profiles and saved/liked/check-in persistence.
6. Add image upload.
7. Add admin review and approval.
8. Add curated journeys.

## Positioning language

Primary line:

> HERE turns the city into a gallery.

Supporting line:

> Discover street art, local artists, creative events, and hidden cultural moments around you.

Longer description:
